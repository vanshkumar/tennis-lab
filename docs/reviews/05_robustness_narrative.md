# Stage 5 narrative and overclaiming review

## Scope

A separate fresh reviewer checked the synthesis, analysis README, generated
report, model-agreement and direct-contrast artifacts, and Stage 3/4 reference
results. Every headline number was reconciled to the artifacts. The review
focused on expected versus actual versus excess upsets, proper-score forecast
difficulty, model-relative favorite definitions, Wimbledon 2022, sample periods,
other-Slam comparisons, and causal grass language.

## Findings resolved

- Replaced the broad claim that Wimbledon “looks more upset-prone” with the
  narrower result that surface-adjusted Elo gives it higher expected rates;
  market and overall-Elo expected contrasts are small and uncertain.
- Reframed the WTA historical result as latest-versus-earliest endpoints for the
  selected Elo forecast, not a monotonic, model-independent rise in intrinsic
  randomness. Worse scores can also reflect forecast drift.
- Labeled the 2020–2022 exclusion as a neutral broad-period sensitivity and
  noted that Australian Open 2020 predates the main disruption.
- Distinguished pre-match extreme-favorite sensitivity from outcome-driven top-
  residual removal.
- Added exact common-sample periods and the absence of Wimbledon 2020 to the
  direct contrast.
- Quantified Wimbledon 2022's larger recent-window influence and smaller
  long-run shift.
- Added Roland Garros and US Open examples showing that “most unusual” depends
  on model, period, and metric.
- Updated stale pending-stage wording and qualified Tennis-Data prices as
  generally the provider's most recent before play, without exact timestamps.
